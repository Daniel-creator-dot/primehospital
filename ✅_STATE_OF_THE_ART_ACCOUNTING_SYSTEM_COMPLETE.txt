                        💡 TYPICAL WORKFLOW
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

REVENUE COLLECTION WORKFLOW:
1. Patient receives service (creates invoice)
2. Patient makes payment
3. Cashier creates Receipt Voucher
4. System creates journal entry:
   Dr: Cash/Bank Account
   Cr: Revenue Account
5. Posts to General Ledger
6. Updates AR if invoice existed
7. Appears in financial reports

EXPENSE PAYMENT WORKFLOW:
1. Department incurs expense
2. Request creates Expense record
3. Manager creates Payment Voucher
4. Approval workflow (Pending → Approved)
5. Accountant marks as Paid
6. System creates journal entry:
   Dr: Expense Account
   Cr: Cash/Bank Account
7. Posts to General Ledger
8. Updates AP if bill existed
9. Appears in financial reports

MONTH-END CLOSING:
1. Review all journal entries (post drafts)
2. Reconcile bank accounts
3. Review AR aging
4. Review AP
5. Run financial reports
6. Verify trial balance (must balance!)
7. Close accounting period
8. Create adjusting entries if needed

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                        🎓 ACCOUNTING PRINCIPLES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DOUBLE-ENTRY BOOKKEEPING:
  Every transaction has TWO sides:
  - Debit (Dr) - Left side
  - Credit (Cr) - Right side
  
  DEBIT increases:  Assets, Expenses
  CREDIT increases: Liabilities, Equity, Revenue
  
  MUST ALWAYS BALANCE: Total Debits = Total Credits

ACCOUNT TYPES:
  Assets:      What you own (Cash, Equipment, AR)
  Liabilities: What you owe (Loans, AP)
  Equity:      Owner's stake (Capital, Retained Earnings)
  Revenue:     Income (Patient Fees, Service Charges)
  Expenses:    Costs (Salaries, Rent, Supplies)

ACCOUNTING EQUATION:
  Assets = Liabilities + Equity
  
  Also:
  Equity = Assets - Liabilities
  Net Income = Revenue - Expenses

NORMAL BALANCES:
  Assets:      Debit
  Liabilities: Credit
  Equity:      Credit
  Revenue:     Credit
  Expenses:    Debit
