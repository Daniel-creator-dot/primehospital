# 📊 Understanding General Ledger Balances

## 🎯 What You're Seeing

The General Ledger report shows **Accounts Receivable** entries for **January 7, 2026**. All entries shown are **DEBITS**, which means they are **increasing** the amount of money owed to your hospital.

---

## 💡 How Balances Work

### **1. Accounts Receivable is an ASSET Account**

- **Asset accounts** (like Accounts Receivable):
  - **DEBIT** = **INCREASES** the balance (money owed to you goes UP)
  - **CREDIT** = **DECREASES** the balance (money paid back goes DOWN)

### **2. Running Balance Calculation**

The "Balance" column shows a **running total** - it adds up each entry as you go down the list.

**Example from your report:**

| Entry | Debit Amount | Running Balance Calculation |
|-------|--------------|----------------------------|
| 1st entry | GHS 44,697.89 | **Balance = 44,697.89** (first entry) |
| 2nd entry | GHS 10,339.30 | **Balance = 44,697.89 + 10,339.30 = 55,037.19** |
| 3rd entry | GHS 6,070.60 | **Balance = 55,037.19 + 6,070.60 = 61,107.79** |
| ... | ... | ... |
| Last entry | ... | **Final Balance = 427,992.31** |

### **3. What the Numbers Mean**

- **Individual Debit Amounts**: Each line shows how much NEW money became owed on that specific transaction
- **Running Balance**: Shows the CUMULATIVE total of all receivables from the start of the report up to that point
- **Final Balance (GHS 427,992.31)**: The total amount of Accounts Receivable shown in this filtered report

### **4. Total Debits (GHS 918,301.31)**

This is the **sum of ALL debit amounts** in the report. It represents:
- **Total new Accounts Receivable created** on January 7, 2026
- This is the total amount of money that became owed to your hospital on that day

---

## 🔍 Why the Balance Increases

Since all entries are **DEBITS** (increasing receivables), the balance keeps going UP:

```
Start: 0.00
+ 44,697.89 (debit) = 44,697.89
+ 10,339.30 (debit) = 55,037.19
+ 6,070.60 (debit) = 61,107.79
... (continues adding)
= Final: 427,992.31
```

---

## 📋 What Each Column Means

1. **Date**: When the transaction happened (all Jan 7, 2026)
2. **Account**: Which account this affects (Corporate AR, Insurance AR)
3. **Description**: Who owes the money (Ghana Comm. Tech. University, etc.)
4. **Reference**: Journal Entry number (JE202601000040, etc.)
5. **Debit**: Amount of NEW receivable created
6. **Credit**: Would show payments (all "-" here means no payments shown)
7. **Balance**: Running total up to that point

---

## ✅ Key Points

1. **All entries are DEBITS** = All are increasing what customers owe you
2. **Running balance** = Adds up as you go down the list
3. **Final balance (427,992.31)** = Total shown in this report
4. **Total debits (918,301.31)** = Sum of all individual debit amounts

---

## 🤔 Common Questions

**Q: Why is the final balance (427,992.31) less than total debits (918,301.31)?**

**A:** The running balance might start from a previous balance, or the report might be filtered to show only certain accounts. The total debits shows ALL new receivables created, while the final balance shows the cumulative total after all entries.

**Q: What if I see a CREDIT entry?**

**A:** A credit would DECREASE the balance. For example:
- If someone pays GHS 10,000, you'd see:
  - Credit: GHS 10,000
  - Balance would go DOWN by 10,000

---

## 📊 Summary

- **Debits** = Money owed to you INCREASES
- **Credits** = Money owed to you DECREASES  
- **Running Balance** = Cumulative total as you go down the list
- **Final Balance** = Total Accounts Receivable shown in this report

The report is showing you all the NEW money that became owed to your hospital on January 7, 2026, and how it accumulates.
